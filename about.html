Our Founder & Director

Dennis Munga Thuku is the Founder and Director of NASHA Organization. Born and raised in Kenya, Dennis is a passionate mental health advocate and a counseling psychology graduate from Meru University of Science and Technology. He is currently pursuing a Master’s degree in Psychology at Kenya Methodist University. With a deep interest in emotional wellness and youth empowerment, Dennis has dedicated his career to creating safe spaces for people to talk about mental health without fear or stigma. His work is driven by personal experiences, academic training, and a strong commitment to social change. Dennis envisions a future where mental health care is accessible, accepted, and prioritized in every community across Kenya.

Co-founder & Deputy Director

Samwel Memusi is the Co-founder and Deputy Director of NASHA Organization. He is a native of Narok County and holds a Bachelor’s degree in Computer Science from Meru University of Science and Technology. Samwel is deeply passionate about community development and youth empowerment, especially in bridging the gap between technology and mental health awareness. Samwel and Dennis met as students at Meru University, where they became close friends through shared interests in leadership, mentorship, and community service. Their late-night conversations about the mental health struggles faced by students and youth in rural Kenya sparked the idea that later became NASHA Organization. Samwel brings a unique tech-driven perspective to NASHA’s mission, helping the organization build digital platforms, awareness campaigns, and data-driven outreach programs. His leadership style is grounded in empathy, innovation, and a strong belief in the power of collaboration to spark change.

About Us

NASHA organization is a community-centered non-profit dedicated to enhancing mental health awareness, advocacy, and support across Kenya. We are committed to destigmatizing mental illness, promoting emotional well-being, and building resilient communities through education, outreach, and accessible mental health services.

our vision

To create a society where poor mental health is easily and effectively handled

our mission

To restore hope among vulnerable children, youth and communities that have been affected by cases of poor mental health and avoid recurrence.
